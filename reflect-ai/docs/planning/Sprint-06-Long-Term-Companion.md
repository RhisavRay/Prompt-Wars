# Sprint 06 — Long-Term Companion

**Sprint:** 06  
**Status:** Planned  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Estimated Duration:** 2–3 Weeks  
**Last Updated:** 30 June 2026

---

# Objective

Transform Reflect from an intelligent journaling application into a long-term companion that helps users reconnect with their own journey.

Sprint 06 focuses on memory, growth, and continuity.

Rather than simply storing journals, Reflect begins helping users revisit meaningful moments, recognize personal growth, and appreciate how their perspective changes over time.

The goal is not to preserve information.

The goal is to preserve stories.

---

# Product Goal

Help users reconnect with earlier versions of themselves.

Reflect should gently remind users that growth often happens gradually and is easiest to recognize when looking backwards.

The application should encourage reflection on progress rather than perfection.

---

# Product Question

> Can Reflect become a trusted long-term companion that remembers without replacing memory?

---

# Core Principle

Memory exists to reconnect.

Not to define.

Reflect should never suggest that a person is permanently one way because of previous journals.

Growth remains possible.

Every journal represents only one moment in a much larger story.

---

# Features

## Feature 1 — Reflection Timeline

**Status:** Not Started

Introduce a chronological timeline of journals and AI reflections.

The timeline should highlight:

- Important moments
- Reflection milestones
- Significant emotional shifts
- Memorable entries

The experience should encourage exploration rather than navigation.

---

## Feature 2 — Looking Back

**Status:** Not Started

Occasionally surface previous journals that may be meaningful today.

Examples:

"One year ago today..."

"You wrote something similar several months ago."

"Your perspective has changed since this journal."

These reminders should always remain optional.

---

## Feature 3 — Personal Milestones

**Status:** Not Started

Celebrate meaningful moments.

Examples:

- First journal
- 50 reflections
- Six months of journaling
- One year with Reflect

Milestones should recognize consistency rather than productivity.

Avoid gamification.

---

## Feature 4 — Growth Stories

**Status:** Not Started

Generate gentle summaries describing personal development over longer periods.

Examples:

"Over the past six months, your journals have gradually shifted from uncertainty toward curiosity."

Growth stories should remain humble.

They are observations—not evaluations.

---

## Feature 5 — Meaningful Memories

**Status:** Not Started

Allow users to bookmark journals that feel personally significant.

Examples:

- Moments of change
- Important decisions
- Personal achievements
- Difficult periods

Bookmarks should become part of the user's personal journey.

---

## Feature 6 — Remembering Together

**Status:** Not Started

Allow Reflect to reference previous journals when generating future reflections.

Examples:

"You mentioned something similar several months ago."

"This reminds me of an earlier journal."

Memory should deepen conversations without becoming repetitive.

Users should always understand why an earlier journal is being referenced.

---

# Success Criteria

Sprint 06 is complete when:

- Users can revisit meaningful moments naturally.
- Reflection timelines feel intuitive.
- Growth summaries feel encouraging rather than evaluative.
- Memory strengthens future reflections.
- Personal milestones celebrate consistency without encouraging competition.
- Reflect feels more like a companion than an archive.

---

# Out of Scope

This sprint intentionally excludes:

- UI redesign
- Performance optimization
- Export tools
- Offline support
- Production hardening
- Beta release preparation

Those belong to the final sprint.

---

# Risks

Potential challenges include:

- Memory becoming repetitive.
- AI recalling irrelevant journals.
- Users feeling overly analyzed.
- Growth summaries sounding deterministic.
- Milestones becoming gamified.

Memory should always serve reflection.

Never engagement metrics.

---

# Dependencies

Requires:

- Completed Sprint 05
- Stable cross-journal insights
- Reflection history
- AI memory retrieval
- Timeline infrastructure

---

# Definition of Done

Every feature must:

- Follow the Reflect Handbook.
- Follow the AI Philosophy.
- Preserve user autonomy.
- Respect user privacy.
- Pass TypeScript.
- Pass ESLint.
- Pass `npm run build`.
- Be manually tested.
- Remain accessible.
- Feel encouraging rather than nostalgic or sentimental.
