# Purpose

This document defines the philosophy behind Reflect.

It explains why Reflect exists, the principles that guide every product decision, and the values that should remain consistent regardless of how the application evolves.

Whenever a product or technical decision is uncertain, this document takes precedence over implementation preferences.

---

# Mission

Reflect exists to help people understand themselves through thoughtful journaling and gentle AI-assisted reflection.

The goal is not to replace introspection with artificial intelligence.

The goal is to make introspection easier.

---

# Vision

Reflect aims to become a trusted companion for personal growth.

Instead of tracking productivity or maximizing engagement, Reflect focuses on helping users recognize emotional patterns, understand themselves more deeply, and build a healthier relationship with their own thoughts over time.

---

# Core Philosophy

Reflect is built on one simple belief:

> People already possess the answers they are searching for.

Reflection helps them discover those answers.

AI simply helps them notice patterns they might otherwise overlook.

---

# Product Principles

## 1. The user always comes first.

Every feature should improve the user's ability to reflect.

Technology exists to support reflection—not to become the focus.

---

## 2. Reflection should feel calm.

The interface should reduce stress rather than create it.

Avoid unnecessary notifications, interruptions, and complexity.

---

## 3. Reflection should feel honest.

Users should never feel pressured to write in a particular way.

There are no "correct" journal entries.

---

## 4. Simplicity is a feature.

Every screen should remove cognitive load rather than add to it.

If a feature requires excessive explanation, reconsider its design.

---

## 5. Privacy is fundamental.

Journals contain deeply personal information.

Reflect should always treat user data with respect, transparency, and strong security practices.

---

## 6. Consistency builds trust.

The application should behave predictably.

Animations, interactions, terminology, and workflows should remain consistent throughout the product.

---

# Emotional Philosophy

Reflect does not ask:

> "What mood are you in?"

Instead, Reflect asks:

> "How are you feeling as you begin today?"

The initial check-in is not intended to perfectly describe the user's emotional state.

It simply captures where the user believes they are starting.

There are no right or wrong answers.

---

# The Role of AI

Artificial intelligence is an assistant—not an authority.

AI should never attempt to replace the user's own interpretation of their experiences.

Instead, AI offers observations that may help the user notice patterns, emotional shifts, recurring themes, or areas for further reflection.

The user remains the final interpreter of their own emotions.

---

# Emotional Journey

Reflect values emotional movement more than emotional labels.

The goal is not to determine whether someone was happy, sad, or anxious.

The goal is to understand how their thoughts and emotions evolve through the act of reflection.

Over time, Reflect should help users recognize progress that may otherwise go unnoticed.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every interaction should feel:

- Calm
- Thoughtful
- Human
- Respectful
- Encouraging

Reflect should never feel clinical, judgmental, or overwhelming.

---

# Success Metrics

Reflect is successful when users:

- Return because journaling feels valuable.
- Better understand themselves over time.
- Feel safe expressing themselves honestly.
- Trust the application's insights without feeling controlled by them.

Reflect is **not** successful because users spend more time inside the application.

Time spent reflecting is more valuable than time spent using the product.

---

# Decision Framework

When evaluating any feature, ask the following questions:

1. Does this reduce or increase cognitive load?
2. Does this help the user reflect more honestly?
3. Does this respect the user's privacy?
4. Does this support the product philosophy?
5. Would this still feel appropriate if AI were removed?

If the answer to any of these questions is "No," reconsider the feature before implementation.

---

# Guiding Statement

> Reflect isn't trying to tell people how they feel.

> Reflect helps people understand how their feelings evolve.

Everything built inside Reflect should reinforce this idea.
